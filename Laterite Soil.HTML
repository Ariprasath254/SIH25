Laterite Soil — Crops, Growth Info & Seed Calculator

Common crops that do well on laterite soils (with proper management & irrigation): plantation crops (cashew, coffee, tea, coconut), tuber crops like cassava (tapioca), and some millets and pulses.

How to use: Choose a crop card below to view its growth duration, ideal climate, seed/planting material rate (per acre) and then use the seed calculator to compute the quantity & cost for the area you plan to sow. Prices may vary by region — you can edit the 'seed price' field to match current local market rates.

Cashew (Anacardium occidentale)

Plant type: Plantation tree • Typical time to bearing: 3–5 years to start producing; full bearing after ~6–8 years.

Climate: Tropical, warm & humid (laterite regions with good drainage). Planting: saplings per acre ~100 (varies by spacing).

Coconut

Plant type: Plantation tree • Time to bearing: 3–6 years for many varieties.

Climate: Tropical, high humidity & rainfall preferred. Planting: seedlings per acre ~120–160 (depends on spacing).

Coffee

Plant type: Plantation shrub/tree • Time to bearing: 3–4 years for many varieties.

Climate: Tropical/subtropical highland areas (well drained laterite slopes). Planting: plants per acre ~400 (varies).

Tea

Plant type: Plantation shrub • Time to bearing: 3–4 years after planting for commercial plucking.

Climate: High rainfall, acidic well drained laterite soils in hilly regions. Planting: bushes per acre vary widely by system.

Cassava / Tapioca (Manihot esculenta)

Plant type: Tuber crop • Time to harvest: 9–12 months (varies with variety & use).

Climate: Tropical, warm; tolerates lateritic soils when well drained. Planting material: ~6800 setts per acre (≈17,000 setts/ha). Source: TNAU.

Finger millet (Ragi)

Plant type: Cereal/Small millet • Time to harvest: 3–4 months (90–120 days).

Climate: Semi-arid to sub-humid; adapts to poorer lateritic soils. Seed rate: ~4 kg/acre (optimum plant population ~1.6–2 lakh plants/acre). Source: millet recommendations.

Pigeon pea (Tur)

Plant type: Pulse • Time to harvest: 4–6 months (varies by variety).

Climate: Warm, semi-arid to sub-humid; many varieties suit drier lateritic tracts. Seed rate: ~6 kg/acre for good yields.
