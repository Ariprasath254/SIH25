What is Black Soil?

Black (regur/chernozem) soils are clayey, high in moisture-retention and rich in certain nutrients — excellent for cotton, oilseeds and several cereals.

Regions: Deccan plateau, parts of Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka.

Crops that grow well in Black Soil

Cotton

Climate: Warm, semi-arid to irrigated. Typical duration: 120–180 days depending on variety.

Recommended seed rate (example): 1.2–2 kg/acre (variety-dependent).

Soybean

Climate: Kharif/rainfed areas; duration: ~80–120 days (variety dependent).

Recommended seed rate: 25–30 kg/acre.

Sunflower

Climate: Rainfed to irrigated; duration: ~85–100 days to maturity.

Typical seed rate (example): 3–6 kg/acre (variety dependent).

Wheat

Climate: Rabi season, irrigated or rainfed; duration: 120–150 days.

Seed rate commonly 36–50 kg/acre (variety dependent).

Millet / Jowar (Sorghum)

Climate: Drought-tolerant, fits black soils under rainfed conditions. Duration: 100–120 days.

Seed rate example: 8–10 kg/acre (variety dependent).

Groundnut

Climate: Warm, rainfed/irrigated. Duration: 120–150 days.

Seed rate example: 50–55 kg/acre (variety dependent).

Pulses (Tur/Gram)

Climate: Rainfed, drought-tolerant. Duration: 120–150 days.

Seed rate example: 10–15 kg/acre (variety dependent).

Sugarcane

Climate: Tropical irrigated areas; long duration (10–18 months). Black soils with good moisture retention are favourable.

Seed material = setts (about 3000–3500 setts per acre).
